The Story

Built the software.
Fell in love with the problem.

When I joined Siemens Healthineers fresh out of engineering college, the job was clear: write reliable software for MRI platforms. Cybersecurity components, update orchestration, license management — real infrastructure that hospitals depend on. I owned features end-to-end and delivered. But two years in, I started asking questions that weren't in my job description.

Why did clinicians work around the feature we'd spent six weeks building? Why did the highest-priority ticket sit untouched for three quarters? Why had nobody spoken to an actual user before we shipped? The gap between what we engineered and what people needed had a name — and it was a product problem, not a technical one.

So I did something about it. I enrolled in Airtribe's 16-week AI-First PM Cohort — not to tick a box, but to learn the discipline behind the craft. The seven projects in this portfolio are what came out: end-to-end PM work across five industries, built from first principles, measured against real outcomes.

Along the way, I co-authored an IEEE paper on ML-based cardiovascular health assessment (IATMSI 2024), and co-led a team to the Top 100 at the AI for Bharat Hackathon 2025 — a cloud-native intelligence platform for Indian farmers, shipped under pressure on AWS. Different domain, same instinct: take the problem seriously, and find the right solution.
